Skyhive generates one floating voxel island per day and archives it forever. The
seed increases by one each day; the generation method keeps improving, so the
islands tell the story of their own pipeline getting better over time.

## How it works

Islands are built by a sequential, composable pipeline — base height, falloff,
detail noise, terracing, voxelisation, surface material, foliage — each step a
small unit you can reorder or extend. Every island's biome, terrain, and features
are derived deterministically from its seed, so the same number always yields the
same world.

## The craft

A Three.js voxel renderer with hex-grid navigation on the front, a Fastify archive
on the back, and a binary `.vox` format tuned to keep each island small. The
interesting constraint: the tag system is append-only, because reordering it would
silently rewrite the history of every island ever generated.
